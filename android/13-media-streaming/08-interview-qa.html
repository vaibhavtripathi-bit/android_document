Interview Q&A — Media & Streaming

30 real questions from Hotstar, Sony LIV, Netflix India, and YouTube engineering rounds — with model L6 answers

Section A — ExoPlayer & Architecture

These come up in every streaming company interview. Know them cold.
1
Why would you choose ExoPlayer over the system MediaPlayer? Sony LIVL5

Short answer: Customizability and format support. MediaPlayer is a black box — you can't change buffering policy, track selection, or error recovery. ExoPlayer exposes every layer as a pluggable interface: TrackSelector, LoadControl, DataSource, Renderer. You can replace any of them.

MediaPlayer also has no native HLS or DASH support — it can only play a direct stream URL. ExoPlayer natively parses HLS manifests, DASH MPDs, and SmoothStreaming, making adaptive streaming a first-class citizen.

Follow-up if asked about Media3: Media3 is the Jetpack repackaging of ExoPlayer that also unifies MediaSession integration and Transformer (transcoding) under one namespace and release train.

2
Walk me through the internal pipeline from setMediaItem() to first frame on screen. HotstarL6
  1. setMediaItem() stores the MediaItem. No network activity yet.
  2. prepare() → ExoPlayer creates a MediaSource (e.g. HlsMediaSource) via MediaSourceFactory.
  3. HlsPlaylistTracker fetches the master M3U8 playlist via DataSource.
  4. DefaultTrackSelector evaluates available renditions against device capabilities and screen size, selects the initial quality.
  5. For fMP4: the init segment (init.mp4) is fetched first. MediaCodec is configured with codec parameters from the init segment.
  6. HlsChunkSource starts fetching media segments. They are queued in the SampleQueue.
  7. MediaCodecVideoRenderer dequeues encoded samples, passes to MediaCodec for decoding.
  8. Decoded frames are rendered to the Surface (from SurfaceView). Audio is rendered to AudioTrack. Video sync uses AudioTrack position as the master clock.
  9. Player transitions: IDLE → BUFFERING → READY. playWhenReady = true starts output.
3
What is the difference between MediaItem and MediaSource? GeneralL5

MediaItem is a declarative descriptor — what to play (URI, MIME type, DRM config, metadata). It carries no operational state and is serializable.

MediaSource is the operational object that actually fetches data and provides samples to renderers. It has a live network connection, parser state, and buffer. ExoPlayer's MediaSourceFactory converts MediaItem → MediaSource at prepare time.

This separation lets the same MediaItem drive an ExoPlayer (local), a Cast session, or a MediaController (remote) — all via the unified Player interface.

4
ExoPlayer crashes with IllegalStateException when called from a background thread. Why, and how do you fix it? GeneralL5

ExoPlayer is single-threaded. All public API calls must be made from the thread the player was created on (typically the main thread). Calling from a background thread violates this contract and throws IllegalStateException in debug builds.

Fix: marshal the call back to the correct thread:

// From background thread:
mainHandler.post { player.play() }

// Or from a coroutine:
withContext(Dispatchers.Main) { player.play() }

Internally, ExoPlayer uses a dedicated playback thread for loading/decoding/rendering, but this is invisible to the caller — you never interact with it directly.

5
Why prefer SurfaceView over TextureView for video rendering? When would you use TextureView? HotstarL5

SurfaceView renders in a dedicated hardware overlay layer — the GPU compositor punches a hole in the View hierarchy and composites the video separately. This means zero GPU compositing cost for the video layer itself.

TextureView is a regular View backed by a SurfaceTexture. The decoded video frames are uploaded as a GPU texture, then composited with the rest of the View tree — adds GPU compositing overhead (memory bandwidth + GPU time).

Use TextureView when: you need to apply View transformations (rotation, arbitrary clip, alpha animations on the video itself). For example: a circular video avatar, or a video that slides in with a shared-element transition.

PlayerView defaults to SurfaceView. Switch via playerView.setVideoSurfaceView() vs setVideoTextureView().

Section B — Protocols & Streaming

Sony LIV and Hotstar ask these heavily in technical rounds.
6
Explain HLS vs DASH. Which does your product use and why? Sony LIVHotstarL5

HLS (HTTP Live Streaming, Apple 2009): manifest = M3U8 (plain text), segments = .ts or .fMP4. Dominant on iOS. Simple, widely deployed. Live detection: absence of #EXT-X-ENDLIST.

DASH (Dynamic Adaptive Streaming over HTTP, MPEG 2011): manifest = MPD (XML), segments = .m4s (fMP4 only). Preferred on Android/Chrome. More expressive: Periods for ad breaks, role descriptors, standard DRM (CENC).

Hotstar uses HLS primarily (large iOS audience). YouTube and Netflix use DASH on Android. Both are supported by ExoPlayer — add media3-exoplayer-hls and media3-exoplayer-dash as dependencies.

Key structural difference: DASH has a three-level hierarchy (Period → AdaptationSet → Representation). HLS is flat (master playlist → rendition playlists). This makes DASH more powerful for multi-period content (ads) but more complex to parse.

7
What is a PSSH box and why does it appear in a DASH manifest? NetflixL6

PSSH = Protection System Specific Header. It is a base64-encoded binary blob inside the MPD's <ContentProtection> element. It contains:

  • The DRM system UUID (Widevine = EDEF8BA9-79D6-4ACE-A3C8-27DCD51D21ED).
  • The Key ID — identifies which content key is required (not the key itself).
  • DRM-specific data the Widevine CDM needs to generate a license request challenge.

When ExoPlayer encounters a PSSH, it passes it to the Widevine CDM, which generates a license challenge. Your app POSTs the challenge to the license server to obtain the content decryption key.

8
A user on a 4G connection starts a video at 1080p but it keeps stuttering. How would you debug this? Sony LIVHotstarL6

Structured debugging approach:

  1. Is it network bandwidth? — Check BandwidthMeter.bitrateEstimate. If it's below 5Mbps for 1080p, the ABR should have downgraded. If it didn't, the TrackSelector might have a setMaxVideoBitrate() cap that's too high.
  2. Is it buffer exhaustion? — Log player.bufferedDuration. If it keeps dropping to 0, the download rate is below playback rate. Check LoadControl parameters — minBufferMs might be too short.
  3. Is it decode performance? — Log VideoFrameMetadataListener.onVideoFrameAboutToBeRendered() for dropped frames. On a low-end device, even 1080p H.264 can exceed real-time decode if the CPU is throttled.
  4. Is it CDN? — Use AnalyticsListener.onLoadCompleted() to measure per-chunk download speeds. If chunks are slow to arrive (not bandwidth-limited), the CDN edge node is overloaded — check if a different CDN region helps.
  5. Is it a discontinuity stall? — An ABR switch between codec-incompatible renditions requires a MediaCodec flush (~200ms). Check if all renditions use the same codec.

Fix: Lower ABR bandwidthFraction from 0.75 to 0.65 to be more conservative. Ensure all renditions use H.264 (not mixing H.264 and HEVC). Tune minDurationForQualityIncreaseMs to prevent premature quality upgrades.

9
How does low-latency live streaming work (LL-HLS)? What is the latency difference from standard HLS? HotstarL6

Standard HLS latency: 15–30 seconds. Why? The player must buffer at least 3 full segments (3 × 6s = 18s) before playback to absorb jitter. Plus segment encoding + CDN propagation time.

LL-HLS latency: 1–3 seconds. Achieved via two mechanisms:

  • Parts: Each segment is subdivided into 200ms "parts" published as #EXT-X-PART tags. The player can start playing before the full segment is finished.
  • Blocking Playlist Reload: Instead of polling the playlist every few seconds, the client sends a request with ?_HLS_msn=43&_HLS_part=2 query params. The server holds the response until that part is published — like long-polling. This eliminates polling latency.

Requires CDN support for CAN-BLOCK-RELOAD=YES in the server's EXT-X-SERVER-CONTROL tag. ExoPlayer supports LL-HLS since Media3 1.0.

10
What happens at an #EXT-X-DISCONTINUITY tag in HLS? Sony LIVL6

A discontinuity marks a timeline break — the continuity of PTS (Presentation Timestamps) is broken. Common causes: ad insertion, stream restart, encoding change.

When ExoPlayer's TsExtractor encounters a discontinuity, it:

  • Resets the PTS offset accumulator — timestamps restart from 0 in the new segment.
  • Flushes the decoder output queue to prevent timestamp confusion.
  • If the codec parameters changed (e.g., different resolution after the ad), flushes and reconfigures MediaCodec.

In your app, Player.Listener.onPositionDiscontinuity() fires with DISCONTINUITY_REASON_AUTO_TRANSITION. Use this to detect ad breaks in SSAI-stitched streams.

Section C — DRM

Netflix and Amazon focus here. Hotstar asks Widevine level questions.
11
What is the difference between Widevine L1 and L3, and what content policies apply to each? NetflixHotstarL5

Widevine L1: All cryptographic operations, decoding, and rendering happen inside a hardware-backed Trusted Execution Environment (TEE). The content decryption key never reaches the Android CPU or app memory. Required for: HD (720p+) and 4K on Netflix, Amazon Prime, Disney+Hotstar. Available on most flagship and mid-range devices.

Widevine L3: Software-only DRM. Decryption happens in a sandboxed software process — the key is in RAM. Considered insecure; limited to SD (480p) by most premium services. Occurs on emulators, rooted devices (TEE attestation fails), or old hardware without TEE.

Check level: MediaDrm(C.WIDEVINE_UUID).getPropertyString("securityLevel") → "L1" or "L3".

12
Walk me through the Widevine license acquisition flow step by step. NetflixL6
  1. ExoPlayer parses the PSSH box from the manifest → gets Key ID.
  2. Creates a MediaDrm session, calls getKeyRequest() to generate a challenge (opaque binary blob proving the device is Widevine-certified and requesting the specific key).
  3. Your app POSTs the challenge to the license server URL, adding your auth token in the Authorization header.
  4. License server verifies: Is the user subscribed? Is the device certificate valid (not revoked)? What playback policies apply?
  5. License server returns an encrypted license (the content key wrapped for this device).
  6. App passes the license response to MediaDrm.provideKeyResponse().
  7. The CDM unwraps and stores the key in the TEE (L1) or encrypted in memory (L3). The key is never accessible to app code.
  8. MediaCodec is initialized with a MediaCrypto object tied to the DRM session. Encrypted samples are decrypted inside the codec/TEE pipeline.
13
A user starts watching offline content and gets a DRM error after 1 hour. What happened and how do you fix it? NetflixL6

The offline license has a license_duration_seconds set to 3600 (1 hour) — shorter than the content duration. When the license expires mid-playback, ExoPlayer throws PlaybackException.ERROR_CODE_DRM_LICENSE_EXPIRED.

Fix:

  • Set license_duration_seconds ≥ content duration + 30 minutes buffer on the license server.
  • Implement proactive license renewal: use OfflineLicenseHelper.getLicenseDurationRemainingSec(keySetId) and schedule a WorkManager task to renew 48 hours before expiry while the device has connectivity.
  • Handle the error in Player.Listener.onPlayerError() — if the device is online, silently fetch a new offline license and call player.prepare() again with the renewed key.

Section D — Live Streaming & ABR

The topic that cost the Sony LIV interview. Know every question here.
14
How does ExoPlayer know a stream is live vs VOD? What is the timeline model difference? Sony LIVL5

HLS detection: Presence/absence of #EXT-X-ENDLIST tag. Without it, ExoPlayer's HlsPlaylistTracker keeps refreshing the playlist to discover new segments.

DASH detection: @type="dynamic" vs @type="static" on the MPD root element.

Timeline difference: VOD → Timeline.Window.isDynamic = false, durationUs is known. Live → isDynamic = true, durationUs = C.TIME_UNSET. The window's defaultPositionUs is set to the target live offset (e.g. 15s behind the live edge).

In UI: player.isCurrentMediaItemLive reads isDynamic. Use it to show/hide the seek bar and "LIVE" badge.

15
Explain EWMA bandwidth estimation. Why does ExoPlayer use two EWMAs and take the minimum? HotstarL6

EWMA = Exponentially Weighted Moving Average. After each chunk download, ExoPlayer computes throughput = bytes × 8 / duration_ms and updates two EWMAs:

  • Fast EWMA (half-life ~3s): reacts quickly to drops.
  • Slow EWMA (half-life ~8s): stable baseline, less noise.

Why min(fast, slow)? When bandwidth drops suddenly (e.g., from 10Mbps to 1Mbps), the fast EWMA immediately shows ~1Mbps while the slow EWMA still reads ~5Mbps. Taking the minimum gives the conservative 1Mbps estimate — preventing the player from selecting a 5Mbps segment that would take 5× longer than expected to download and cause a stall.

This asymmetry is intentional: be pessimistic on degradation, require both EWMAs to converge before upgrading.

16
How would you implement a "data saver" feature that caps video quality at 480p on mobile data? HotstarL6

Two approaches:

Option A — TrackSelector parameters (simple, recommended):

val isMetered = !connectivityManager.getNetworkCapabilities(activeNetwork)
    ?.hasCapability(NET_CAPABILITY_NOT_METERED)

trackSelector.parameters = trackSelector.buildUponParameters()
    .setMaxVideoSize(
        if (isMetered) 854 else Int.MAX_VALUE,
        if (isMetered) 480 else Int.MAX_VALUE
    )
    .setMaxVideoBitrate(if (isMetered) 1_000_000 else Int.MAX_VALUE)
    .build()

Option B — Custom TrackSelection.Factory (more control):

Subclass ExoTrackSelection.Factory, override createTrackSelections(), filter the format list to height ≤ 480 when on cellular, and return an AdaptiveTrackSelection over the filtered set. Register the custom factory with DefaultTrackSelector.

Also register a ConnectivityManager.NetworkCallback to update the selector when the user switches between WiFi and mobile data mid-session.

17
Hotstar serves 25M concurrent viewers during IPL. What are the biggest streaming infrastructure challenges? HotstarL7
  • CDN edge saturation: Millions of manifest + segment requests per second. Edge nodes cache segments but must also serve 404s for segments not yet encoded. CDN hit rate must be >99% to protect origin.
  • ABR with unreliable bandwidth estimates: At peak, bandwidth estimates reflect CDN congestion, not last-mile capacity. Server-side bitrate hints (embedded in manifest) help the player cap quality sensibly.
  • Manifest refresh load: 25M × 1 request/3s = 8M manifest requests/second. Manifests must be CDN-cached with TTL = targetDuration/2. Never let manifests bypass the CDN.
  • DNS / TCP warm-up: On app launch, millions of devices simultaneously resolve CDN hostnames. Use DNS prefetching (HTTPS DNS-over-HTTPS) and pre-warming connections via OkHttp.newCall(request).enqueue() on app start.
  • Low-latency live: LL-HLS reduces latency but requires server-push infrastructure. At scale, blocking playlist reload (long-polling) must be handled by the origin without blocking threads — requires async I/O servers (Nginx + Lua, or custom Go/Kotlin server).
  • Device diversity: India market has wide range of Android devices (2GB RAM, Snapdragon 400 chips). ABR must account for decode performance, not just bandwidth.

Section E — Offline Downloads

18
What are the four things that must happen for "Download for Offline" to work with DRM content? NetflixL5
  1. Segment download: All video, audio, and subtitle segments saved to local storage via DownloadManager.
  2. Manifest storage: HLS/DASH manifest saved locally so ExoPlayer can reconstruct the stream without network.
  3. Offline DRM license pre-fetch: OfflineLicenseHelper.downloadLicense(mediaItem) fetches a license where the key is stored on-device (TEE for L1, encrypted storage for L3). Returns a keySetId — save this for playback.
  4. Cache-first playback routing: CacheDataSource.Factory with SimpleCache as the primary source. ExoPlayer reads segments from cache, never hits the network.
19
Why should DRM-protected downloads be stored in internal storage, not on an SD card? NetflixL6

Widevine DRM keys are device-bound. When an offline license is issued, the content key is encrypted with a device-specific key derived from the TEE's hardware root of trust. If the user copies encrypted segments to another device (e.g., by moving an SD card), the other device's Widevine CDM cannot decrypt them — the license won't work.

Additionally, external storage is accessible via USB in some configurations, making the encrypted segments easier to extract for offline analysis. Internal storage (filesDir) is sandboxed to your app.

For non-DRM content (podcasts, public domain videos), external storage is fine — getExternalFilesDir() is the appropriate location.

Section F — MediaSession & Background Playback

20
Explain the difference between MediaSessionService and a plain Service for audio playback. GeneralL5

MediaSessionService is a specialised Service subclass that the Android system recognises as a media playback service. Benefits over a plain Service:

  • Automatic media notification management (play/pause/skip buttons, seek bar on Android 13+).
  • System can rebind to it when a Bluetooth device connects, even if the app is in the background.
  • Participates in Android's "Now Playing" API — Google Assistant can query and control it.
  • Android Auto and Wear OS connect to it automatically via SessionToken.

A plain Service requires you to build all of this manually — media notification, BT reconnection, Auto integration — which is error-prone and misses edge cases.

21
What happens if you forget to set handleAudioFocus = true in ExoPlayer? GeneralL5

Without audio focus management, your app plays audio simultaneously with other apps — including during phone calls. The system will still route audio through your app even if a call comes in.

Set handleAudioFocus = true in ExoPlayer.Builder. ExoPlayer will then:

  • Request AUDIOFOCUS_GAIN on play() — pausing other media apps.
  • Pause on AUDIOFOCUS_LOSS (phone call, another app taking focus).
  • Duck (lower volume) on AUDIOFOCUS_LOSS_TRANSIENT_CAN_DUCK (navigation instruction).
  • Resume on AUDIOFOCUS_GAIN regained.

This is a guaranteed 1-star review if missed in production.

Section G — System Design

These are Staff/L6 system design questions. Structure your answer: requirements → components → trade-offs.
22
Design the Android video player architecture for Hotstar's IPL live stream. HotstarDesignL6

Key requirements: Low latency (commentary sync), 25M concurrent, wide device range, background playback, Chromecast, pip mode, offline not applicable for live.

Architecture:

  • Player: ExoPlayer (Media3) with LL-HLS source. Target live offset: 8s. Playback speed adjustment: 0.97–1.03×.
  • ABR: Conservative — bandwidthFraction = 0.65 (more headroom for CDN jitter). Max quality capped server-side via manifest bitrate hints.
  • Service: MediaSessionService for background audio (commentary) when video goes to background. PiP mode handled via Activity.enterPictureInPictureMode() on user swipe.
  • Network: OkHttp DataSource with connection pooling. DNS prefetch on app launch. CDN failover: try primary CDN, fall back to secondary on 3 consecutive segment failures.
  • Quality switch strategy: All renditions must be H.264 (same codec) to enable seamless ABR switches. HEVC disabled for live (reduces codec-mismatch stalls).
  • Resilience: On network loss, show "Reconnecting..." overlay. On reconnect, seek to live edge (player.seekToDefaultPosition()).
  • Analytics: AnalyticsListener — log rebuffer events, quality switches, startup time. Send to backend every 30s.
23
How would you implement a "Continue Watching" feature that resumes precisely where the user left off, across devices? Sony LIVDesignL6

On-device: Save position to DataStore (Preferences DataStore) every 5 seconds via a coroutine launched in the ViewModel. Key: contentId → positionMs.

Cross-device sync: On pause/stop, POST { contentId, positionMs, deviceId, timestamp } to your backend. On app open, fetch the latest position from backend (server is source of truth). Merge strategy: take whichever is more recent by timestamp.

Restoring in ExoPlayer:

player.setMediaItem(mediaItem, savedPositionMs)
player.prepare()

Edge cases: If saved position is within 30s of content end → start from beginning (completed). If content has been removed → handle 404 gracefully, clear saved position. If position is stale (>30 days) → start from beginning.

DRM consideration: Position sync is unrelated to DRM. DRM license is fetched fresh on each play session. The saved position is just a timestamp in milliseconds.

24
How does Chromecast integration work with ExoPlayer? HotstarDesignL6

The Cast SDK provides a CastPlayer that implements the same Player interface as ExoPlayer. Your UI code doesn't change — you swap the player implementation:

// When Cast session starts:
binding.playerView.player = castPlayer  // same PlayerView, different Player impl

// When Cast session ends:
binding.playerView.player = exoPlayer   // resume local playback

Architecture: Your app (the "sender") sends a MediaInfo object to the Cast receiver (a Chrome browser on the TV). The receiver runs a Web Receiver app that plays the stream. The sender only controls playback (play, pause, seek) via Cast protocol over WiFi.

Position handoff: When ending a Cast session, read castPlayer.currentPosition and resume ExoPlayer at that position. Same for the reverse — when starting Cast, pass the current position in MediaInfo.streamPosition.

DRM: The Cast receiver needs its own Widevine license — it is a separate device. Your license server must issue a license for the Cast device's Widevine credentials. The sender app does not handle the Cast device's DRM.

Section H — Quick-Fire (Last 10 Minutes of Interview)

25
What is the Widevine UUID?

EDEF8BA9-79D6-4ACE-A3C8-27DCD51D21ED. Use C.WIDEVINE_UUID in ExoPlayer/Media3.

26
What does #EXT-X-ENDLIST mean in HLS?

The stream is complete (VOD). Without it, the playlist is live and will be refreshed by the player periodically to discover new segments.

27
What is the difference between Player.STATE_BUFFERING and Player.STATE_IDLE?

IDLE: player has not been prepared (no MediaItem loaded, or after stop() called). BUFFERING: player is prepared and trying to accumulate enough buffer to play, but has not reached the minimum threshold yet. The spinner in your UI should show on BUFFERING, not IDLE.

28
What does bandwidthFraction = 0.75 mean in AdaptiveTrackSelection?

ExoPlayer will only select a quality track whose bitrate is ≤ 75% of the estimated bandwidth. This 25% safety margin absorbs estimation noise and CDN jitter, preventing the player from selecting a track that consumes the full estimated bandwidth and risks stalling.

29
Why call player.release() in onStop() instead of onDestroy()?

On API 24+, onStop() is called when the app goes to the background (multi-window mode too). Releasing in onDestroy() would leak a MediaCodec decoder while the app is backgrounded — Android has a limited hardware decoder pool (typically 2–3). Release in onStop() and re-initialize in onStart(), restoring the saved position.

30
What is DownloadHelper used for?

DownloadHelper prepares a download request by first fetching the manifest and analyzing available tracks. This lets you inspect what quality levels, audio languages, and subtitle tracks are available before committing to a download. Without it, DownloadManager downloads all tracks at all qualities — potentially many GB. Use DownloadHelper to select a specific quality (e.g., 720p + Hindi audio) and build a targeted DownloadRequest.
