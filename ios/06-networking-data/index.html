Series 6: Networking & Data Persistence

From URLSession and TLS through Core Data, SwiftData, Keychain, and the sandbox — the end-to-end story of how iOS moves and stores data safely at scale.

At Staff level, interviewers stop asking whether you can call URLSession.shared.dataTask and start asking how your stack behaves under load, how you isolate secrets, and how persistence choices affect crash recovery, migrations, and multi-device sync. Networking and data persistence are where security, performance, and product requirements collide: a wrong cache policy leaks PII, a blocking main-context save janks the UI, and a naive Keychain accessibility choice breaks background refresh. This series is written for Google L6 / Staff-style depth — configuration trade-offs, framework internals, failure modes, and the system-design questions that decide whether you own the platform layer for a team or a product line.

Documents in This Series

01 — URLSession Architecture Deep Dive

URLSession configuration, task hierarchy, HTTP/2 multiplexing, URLProtocol and custom stacks, URLCache behavior, TLS and App Transport Security, certificate pinning, background transfers, and WebSocket integration.

02 — Core Data Stack & Concurrency

NSManagedObjectContext patterns, NSPersistentStoreCoordinator, NSPersistentContainer, perform/performAndWait, NSFetchedResultsController, lightweight vs heavyweight migration, batch operations, CloudKit sync, and faulting.

03 — SwiftData Framework

The @Model macro, ModelContainer and ModelContext, @Query, modeling relationships, migrations, comparison with Core Data, and current framework limitations.

04 — Keychain & Security

Keychain Services API, kSecAttrAccessible and data protection classes, biometric-gated items, Keychain sharing between apps and extensions, CryptoKit primitives, App Attest, and DeviceCheck.

05 — File System & Sandbox

Application sandbox layout, file protection classes, FileManager usage, App Groups for shared containers, iCloud document and ubiquity patterns, and UIDocument lifecycle.

06 — Serialization & Encoding

Codable synthesis and custom coding, JSONEncoder/JSONDecoder and property list encoders, performance characteristics, Protocol Buffers, FlatBuffers, and MessagePack on Apple platforms.

07 — Interview Questions

50+ L6-level questions with deep answers spanning URLSession, caching, Core Data concurrency, SwiftData, Keychain, sandboxing, serialization, and cross-topic system design.

What You'll Master

  • Networking stack: How sessions, tasks, delegates, and protocols compose; when to customize caching and TLS; how background URLSession differs from in-process networking.
  • Apple persistence models: Core Data's context hierarchy and merge policies versus SwiftData's container/context model and macro-driven schema.
  • Secrets and trust: Keychain accessibility semantics, secure enclave integration, pinning strategies, and fraud signals (App Attest, DeviceCheck).
  • OS boundaries: Sandbox paths, protection classes, App Groups, and how file-backed state interacts with iCloud and extensions.
  • Wire and disk formats: Codable cost and correctness; when binary schemas (Protobuf, FlatBuffers) beat JSON for mobile payloads.
  • Interview narrative: Articulating trade-offs, failure modes, and migration stories the way a Staff engineer would in a design review.
